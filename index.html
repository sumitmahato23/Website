Introduction

About Me

I'm a PhD candidate in Environmental Engineering at the University of Cincinnati, advised by Dr. Dongmei Feng. My research sits at the intersection of remote sensing, machine learning, and water quality — using satellite imagery to monitor and model Harmful Algal Blooms (HABs) at continental scale.

"Building one of the first continental-scale Chl-a models for U.S. rivers using Sentinel-2 imagery and USGS/EPA field data — NSE = 0.87."

Prior to my PhD, I worked as a Water Resource Engineer in Nepal, designing climate-resilient water supply infrastructure for communities in Ramechhap and Sindhuli districts. I also hold a B.S. in Civil Engineering from Tribhuvan University.

Outside research, I'm active in campus leadership — serving as President of the Nepalese Student Association, Vice President of Graduate Student Government, and founding President of the ASPRS UC Student Chapter.

Large-Scale Chlorophyll-a Modeling in U.S. Rivers
Aug 2023 – Jan 2026  ·  Remote Sensing & ML · UC
Built one of the first continental-scale Chl-a models for the contiguous U.S. using Sentinel-2 imagery and USGS/EPA field data. Developed Python and R pipelines for all 21 U.S. HUC regions.
